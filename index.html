Phishing Awareness Training Module

Phishing is a type of online scam where criminals pretend to be trusted people or companies, such as banks, online services, or coworkers. They try to trick you into sharing your password, bank details, or downloading harmful software. Always check emails, messages, and links carefully before clicking.

Understanding Social Engineering

Attackers usually do not hack computers directly they trick people instead. Phishing emails use emotions like fear, excitement, or urgency to make you act without thinking. Learning to recognize these tricks is the best way to protect yourself. Always take a moment to check who sent the message before clicking any links or attachments. If something looks suspicious or too good to be true, it is safer to ignore it or verify it with the sender.

⏱️

Urgency

"Act within 24 hours or your account will be suspended." Pressure to act fast prevents victims from verifying legitimacy.

⚠️

Fear

"Unauthorized login detected on your account." Fear of loss or compromise triggers panic-driven clicks.

🏛️

Authority

Impersonating IT departments, banks, or executives exploits our natural tendency to comply with authority figures.

🎯

Scarcity

"Only 2 spots left — claim your refund now." Limited-time framing pushes victims to bypass due diligence.

🛡️ Defensive Checklist: 4 Actionable Best Practices

  • ✔
    Verify the channel. If an email asks for urgent action, contact the company directly using a phone number or website you already trust — never a link or number provided in the email itself.
  • ✔
    Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA). Even if your password is stolen, MFA adds a critical second barrier attackers usually can't bypass.
  • ✔
    Analyze links before clicking. Hover to preview the destination URL, and check for misspellings, extra subdomains, or unfamiliar top-level domains.
  • ✔
    Slow down under pressure. Urgency is a manufactured tactic. Legitimate organizations rarely demand instant action via email — pause and verify before acting.
